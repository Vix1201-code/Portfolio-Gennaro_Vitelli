What is the Agenda 2030?

The Agenda 2030 for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, now and into the future. It is built around 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which are an urgent call to action for ending poverty, protecting the planet, and ensuring prosperity for all by 2030. These goals are integrated and balance the three dimensions of sustainable development: the economic, social, and environmental.

Project 2: 13th point in the Agenda 2030

One of the key goals of the Agenda 2030 is addressing climate change and its impacts. Goal 13 focuses on taking urgent action to combat climate change and its effects. This involves enhancing the capacity of countries to adapt to climate change, improve climate education, and implement global environmental agreements. During my PCTO experience at NetCom, I worked on developing a dashboard to monitor meteorological data, which directly ties into this goal. The project helped me understand the importance of real-time data in forecasting and addressing climate issues, making it an essential part of contributing to the achievement of Goal 13.
